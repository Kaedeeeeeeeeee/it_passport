## Standard Exam Fee

The standard exam fee for the IT Passport exam is **7,500 yen (tax included)** (as of the 2024 revision). This amount can be a significant burden for high school and university students. If you fail, you must pay the fee again each time you retake the exam, so passing on your first attempt is crucial.

## Main Discount Programs

### 1. Student Discount (via School)

The student discount is a system where schools contract with the IPA and issue **discount coupons (vouchers)**. Eligible institutions include high schools, vocational schools, and universities. Vouchers are distributed from the school to individual students. The discount is applied by entering a coupon code during registration. The discount amount varies by school, with some cases offering the exam for free or at roughly half price.

### 2. Corporate Vouchers

Corporate vouchers are a system where companies purchase vouchers in bulk for their employees. They are often distributed as part of internal training programs, and many IT and consulting firms have adopted this system.

### 3. Job Support Seminar Perks

Some job-hunting agencies offer "IT Passport exam fee support." However, there are conditions for use, such as registering with the agency and attending an interview.

### 4. Local Government and Public Institution Support

Some local governments and vocational training schools provide subsidies for exam fees. Additionally, vocational training programs through Hello Work (public employment security centers) may include the exam fee.

## How to Check for Student Discounts

### For High School Students

If you are a high school student, first consult with your guidance counselor. Schools with commercial or information technology departments are more likely to offer student discounts, so there is a good chance you can use one.

### For University Students

University students should inquire at their career center or the office of their information technology department. The student union may also coordinate discounts in some cases.

### For Vocational School Students

Vocational school students often find that the exam fee is already included in their tuition structure at the time of enrollment. It is a good idea to check with the academic affairs office.

## Benefits of Using a Student Discount

Using a student discount can significantly save you the 7,500 yen exam fee. Another major advantage is that it reduces the cost of retaking the exam if you fail. Furthermore, since the entire school often takes the exam together, it becomes easier to find study partners.

## Points to Note

### Vouchers Require Advance Application

Vouchers are applied by entering a coupon code during registration; they cannot be applied at the time of payment on the exam day. Be sure to complete the application process in advance.

### Vouchers Have Expiration Dates

Vouchers often have an expiration date. It is recommended to use them promptly after receiving them.

### Vouchers Cannot Be Purchased Individually

Vouchers are a system intended for schools and companies; individuals cannot purchase them.

## Other Cost-Saving Tips

### Reduce Textbook Costs

One reference book (1,500 to 2,500 yen) and free past exam question websites are sufficient for preparation. Consider using used books or borrowing from the library.

### Use Free Practice Exam Sites

The [practice exams](/exam) on this site are also available for free. By conducting trial runs in a format similar to the actual exam, you can avoid unnecessary retakes.
