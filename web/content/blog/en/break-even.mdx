## What Is the Break-Even Point?

The break-even point is the sales revenue at which total revenue equals total costs, resulting in zero profit. If sales exceed this point, a profit is generated; if they fall below, a loss occurs. This is a frequently tested topic in the IT Passport exam, with calculation problems appearing regularly.

## Essential Terminology

Variable costs are expenses that increase or decrease in proportion to sales revenue, such as material costs and sales commissions. Fixed costs are expenses that remain constant regardless of sales revenue, including rent, base salaries, and depreciation. Contribution margin is the amount remaining after subtracting variable costs from sales revenue, serving as the source for covering fixed costs and generating profit. The contribution margin ratio is the contribution margin divided by sales revenue, and it equals 1 minus the variable cost ratio (variable costs ÷ sales revenue).

## Break-Even Point Formula

```
Break-Even Sales Revenue = Fixed Costs ÷ Contribution Margin Ratio
```

Or

```
Break-Even Sales Revenue = Fixed Costs ÷ (1 − Variable Cost Ratio)
```

## Calculation Example

Consider a case with sales revenue of 10 million yen, variable costs of 6 million yen, and fixed costs of 3 million yen. The variable cost ratio is 6 ÷ 10 = 0.6 (60%), and the contribution margin ratio is 1 − 0.6 = 0.4 (40%). Using these figures, the break-even sales revenue is 3 million yen (fixed costs) divided by 0.4 (contribution margin ratio), resulting in 7.5 million yen. At sales of 7.5 million yen, profit is zero, while at the actual sales of 10 million yen, profit is 1 million yen.

## What Is CVP Analysis?

CVP analysis is a method for examining the relationship between Cost, Volume, and Profit. The break-even point is a key output of this analysis, allowing you to estimate "how much you need to sell to make a profit" or "what sales revenue is needed to achieve a target profit."

## Target Profit Sales Revenue

```
Target Profit Sales Revenue = (Fixed Costs + Target Profit) ÷ Contribution Margin Ratio
```

For example, to achieve a target profit of 2 million yen in the previous scenario, you would need sales revenue of (3 million yen fixed costs + 2 million yen target profit) divided by 0.4, which equals 12.5 million yen.

Additionally, solving two or three example problems by hand will help you answer confidently on the actual exam.
