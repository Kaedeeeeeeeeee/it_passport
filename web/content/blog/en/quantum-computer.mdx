## What Is a Quantum Computer?

A quantum computer is a new type of computing device that uses the principles of quantum mechanics. While classical computers calculate using bits (0 or 1), quantum computers use qubits. For certain problems, they can vastly outperform classical computers—a phenomenon known as quantum supremacy.

## The Three Major Principles of Quantum Computers

### Superposition

Due to the principle of superposition, a single qubit can represent both 0 and 1 simultaneously. Using N qubits allows for the simultaneous calculation of 2^N states, dramatically expanding the potential for parallel processing.

### Quantum Entanglement

Quantum entanglement is a phenomenon where multiple qubits become linked and exhibit strong correlations. When one qubit is observed, the state of the other is instantly determined. Research is underway on high-speed computation methods that leverage this property.

### Observation

When an observation is made, the superposition collapses into a definite value. Therefore, executing calculations on a quantum computer requires special algorithms that account for the effects of observation.

## Expected Application Fields

### Cryptanalysis

In the field of cryptanalysis, Shor's algorithm has gained attention for speeding up prime factorization. Since current RSA encryption could potentially be broken, active research is being conducted on post-quantum cryptography (PQC).

### Optimization Problems

Applications are expected in practical optimization problems such as delivery route optimization, financial portfolio construction, and scheduling. In particular, more efficient solutions are being explored for NP-hard problems like the traveling salesman problem.

### Quantum Chemistry Calculations

Quantum chemistry calculations can accurately simulate molecular behavior, potentially revolutionizing new drug development and the design of high-performance materials.

### Machine Learning

In the machine learning field, a technique called quantum machine learning (QML) is under research, and it is expected to accelerate the processing of large datasets.

## Types of Quantum Computers

There are two main types of quantum computers. The gate-based model is being developed by major IT companies like IBM, Google, and Microsoft, and is capable of general-purpose computation. Meanwhile, the annealing model is led by D-Wave Systems and delivers performance specialized for optimization problems.

## Current Challenges

Several challenges remain for the practical use of quantum computers. First, qubits are highly susceptible to external noise and have high error rates. Additionally, the number of qubits currently available is still small, and it is estimated that one million qubits are needed for practical applications. Furthermore, stable qubit operation requires an ultra-low temperature environment near absolute zero, making specialized cooling equipment essential.

## Quantum Cryptography

Quantum cryptography is a cryptographic communication technology that applies the principles of quantum mechanics to detect eavesdropping. A representative protocol is BB84, which uses the changes caused by observing quantum states to verify whether eavesdropping has occurred.
