## Why the IT Passport Is a Good Fit for Stay-at-Home Parents

### A Weapon for Re-Employment

The IT Passport is a national certification, so you can list it on your resume. Holders tend to be viewed favorably in IT and clerical job postings, and it serves as effective proof of your willingness to learn when restarting your career after a break.

### Obtainable from Home in Short Time

Since it's a year-round CBT exam, you can choose your exam date to fit your schedule. You can study entirely on your own without attending a school, and the exam fee is a reasonable 7,500 yen (tax included).

### IT Knowledge Will Be Essential Going Forward

With programming education becoming mandatory for children, having basic knowledge helps parents guide them. It also opens up options for side jobs and remote work, and helps you understand household budget apps and smart home systems.

## Sample Daily Schedule for a Stay-at-Home Parent

| Time Slot | Activity |
| --- | --- |
| 7:00–7:30 AM | 5 past exam questions on your phone while preparing breakfast |
| 1:00–1:30 PM | 1 chapter of a reference book while the child naps |
| 9:00–9:30 PM | 10 past exam questions after putting the child to bed |

Total: 90 minutes per day, approximately 130 hours over 3 months

## Advantages of Studying from Home

No commuting or school fees are required; self-study only costs 1,000–3,000 yen for materials. You can flexibly secure study time according to your health or family schedule, and you can use audio materials or study in short bursts while doing housework.

## Learning Resources

### Reference Books

One book is enough. Browse at a bookstore and choose one that's easy for you to read. The "Kitami-style" and "Easiest" series are popular choices.

### Past Exam Apps

Smartphone apps let you practice in spare moments. Also be sure to use this site's [past exam list](/exams) and [practice exams](/exam).

### Video Materials (Optional)

YouTube has many free explanatory videos. Paid courses on platforms like Udemy are also available starting from around 1,500 yen.

## Success Patterns for Stay-at-Home Parents

### Pattern A: Pass in 3 Months

A full-time stay-at-home parent with young children studied 1 hour a day for 3 months. They passed by steadily accumulating study time between household chores.

### Pattern B: Pass Comfortably in 6 Months

A full-time stay-at-home parent with multiple children studied 5 hours per week and passed with a relaxed pace over six months.

## How to Leverage It for Your Career

### Remote Work

With basic IT knowledge, you can expand your options on cloud-sourcing platforms for data entry, web writing, or e-commerce management support.

### Temp Work or Part-Time Jobs

Adding IT skills to general clerical work can lead to a higher hourly wage. Some job postings even list "IT Passport holder" as a requirement.

### Returning to Full-Time Employment

In the market for stay-at-home parents in their 30s and 40s returning to work, being able to demonstrate a continuous learning attitude is a major strength.

## Tips for Staying Motivated

### Get Your Family on Board

Set small rewards, like going out to eat with the kids after passing, and temporarily ask your family to help with household chores.

### Build Small Successes

Aim to make progress even if it's just one page a day, and let go of perfectionism. The passing line is 60%.

### Visualize Progress with Past Question Platforms

Using past question platforms to see your accuracy rate rise day by day makes it easier to maintain motivation.
