## Conclusion: The IT Passport Is Useful Even for Civil Servants

The IT Passport does not directly qualify you to pass national or local government civil service exams. However, an increasing number of municipalities are adding bonus points or evaluating it. As digital transformation (DX) in government work becomes essential, IT knowledge is increasingly required, making it well worth obtaining.

## Evaluation in National Government Exams

### Comprehensive and General Positions

In exams for comprehensive and general positions, there are basically no bonus points for the IT Passport. However, it may be considered in post-hire performance evaluations or job assignments. This can be especially advantageous if you are aiming for IT-related departments such as the Digital Agency.

### Specialized Positions (Information Processing)

For positions like National Tax Specialist or Financial Specialist, IT knowledge tends to be emphasized. Having the Fundamental Information Technology Engineer Examination (FE) in addition to the IT Passport makes you more likely to be evaluated favorably.

### Self-Defense Forces and Police

Cybersecurity divisions in the Self-Defense Forces and police tend to value IT-related certifications. The IT Passport can serve as a stepping stone to build a career path toward higher-level certifications like the FE.

## Evaluation in Local Government Exams

### Municipalities with Bonus Point Systems

Major cities such as Tokyo, Yokohama, and Osaka have introduced bonus point systems for IT-related certifications. The bonus typically ranges from 1 to 10 points, added to a total score of 200 to 300 points. Be sure to check the exam guidelines of the municipality you plan to apply to for eligible certifications.

### Examples of Eligible Certifications for Bonus Points

Main certifications eligible for bonus points include the IT Passport, FE, Information Security Management, and Applied Information Technology Engineer Examination (AP). Eligible certifications vary by municipality, so check the latest information.

### Municipalities Without Bonus Point Systems

Even in municipalities without a bonus point system, having the IT Passport can work in your favor during interviews or personal evaluations. Obtaining it demonstrates your willingness to learn and your IT literacy.

## How to Leverage It in Government Work

### DX Promotion Departments

In local government DX promotion departments, you may be tasked with formulating and executing municipal DX promotion plans. The knowledge gained from the IT Passport is useful for digitizing administrative procedures and operating the My Number system.

### Information Security

Knowledge from the IT Passport is essential for managing the security of municipal information systems and protecting personal data. Understanding the [basics of the Personal Information Protection Law](/blog/personal-info) directly applies to practical work.

### IT Procurement and Contracts

As the party ordering system development, managing vendor contracts and requirements definitions requires IT Passport knowledge. Understanding the [flow of system development processes](/blog/sdlc-process) enables proper ordering.

### General Administrative Work

IT skills directly boost productivity in general administrative tasks, such as improving efficiency with Excel and email. Understanding business systems increases efficiency in daily operations.

## Government DX Trends

### Establishment of the Digital Agency (2021)

The Digital Agency was established in 2021, creating a national-level DX promotion framework. This has led to increased mid-career recruitment of IT personnel.

### Municipal DX Promotion Plan

The Ministry of Internal Affairs and Communications' "Municipal DX Promotion Plan," formulated in 2020, has made securing IT personnel an urgent task for each municipality. The IT Passport is a useful first step in this direction.

### My Number Card and Administrative Digitalization

The use of the My Number system is advancing, with administrative procedures for childcare, nursing care, and more being moved online. IT Passport knowledge helps in understanding this digitalization.

## Timing for Aspiring Civil Servants to Obtain the IT Passport

### Before the Exam

Obtaining the IT Passport as a student and listing it on your resume can be a strong selling point. For details, see [IT Passport study methods for high school and college students](/blog/study-students).

### After Hiring

It is also common to obtain it as part of self-development after joining an organization. Many people take the exam in combination with in-house training, gaining knowledge directly applicable to their work.

### Steps Toward Higher-Level Certifications

It is recommended to progress step by step from the IT Passport to the FE and then to Information Security Management. You can compare the differences between the two in [IT Passport vs. FE](/blog/it-vs-fe).

## Points to Note in Civil Service Exams

### Systems Vary by Municipality

Since systems vary greatly by municipality, be sure to check the latest exam guidelines for the municipality you are applying to. Systems from previous years may have changed.

### How to Talk About It in Interviews

In interviews, prepare to explain specifically why you obtained the IT Passport and how you plan to use it in government work. It is important to emphasize not just that you "have" it, but that you can "apply" it.
