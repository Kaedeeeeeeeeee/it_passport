## Can You Pass in Two Weeks?

Passing in two weeks is entirely possible if you can dedicate 4 to 5 hours per day for 14 days. With a total of about 60 hours of study time, you can cover the exam scope. This plan is ideal for periods like long school breaks or consecutive paid vacation days. For working professionals, a realistic goal is to secure about 50 hours by studying 8 hours on each of four weekend days and 2 hours on each of ten weekdays.

## The Two-Week Strategic Principles

There are three core principles for this study strategy. First, keep input to a minimum: prepare just one reference book and only check the key points. Second, center your learning on past exam questions: solidify your knowledge by solving problems and making mistakes. Third, focus on frequently tested topics and have the courage to abandon minor areas.

## The 14-Day Schedule

### Days 1–3: Ultra-Fast Input

Days 1 through 3 are for ultra-fast input. Go through your reference book once over these three days, but don't dig deep—just follow the bolded text. Don't worry about memorizing precise definitions yet; the important thing is to grasp the overall picture first.

### Days 4–10: Topic-Based Past Exam Practice

From Day 4 to Day 10, work on past exam questions organized by topic. Aim for a pace of 50 questions per day, targeting a total of 350 questions over seven days. During each day's practice, make a note of five new terms you encounter—this will help with review.

### Days 11–13: Mock Exams + Weak Point Review

Days 11 through 13 are for mock exams and reviewing your weak areas. Take one full set of mock exam questions each day, then spend 1–2 hours reviewing and checking the explanations. For your weak subjects, use the [Strategy Summary](/category/strategy), [Management Summary](/category/management), and [Technology Summary](/category/technology) pages for focused practice. You can access the mock exams [here](/exam).

### Day 14: Final Adjustments

Day 14 is for final adjustments. Do a last review of any troublesome terms, and double-check the exam location and what to bring. Also, refer to [What to Expect on CBT Exam Day](/blog/exam-day-cbt). Most importantly, go to bed early to ensure you get enough sleep.

## Key Focus Areas (Based on Data from the Last 5 Years)

Based on exam trends from the past five years, it's crucial to focus on key areas. Security appears in 8–9 questions per year; be sure to understand [cyber attacks](/blog/cyber-attacks), [encryption](/blog/encryption-basics), and [authentication](/blog/auth-vs-authz). AI/IoT accounts for 5–10 questions, centering on [AI and machine learning](/blog/ai-ml-basics) and [What is IoT?](/blog/iot-toha). For networking, the [OSI model](/blog/osi-model) and [TCP/IP](/blog/tcp-ip) are frequent topics, while for databases/SQL, [relational databases](/blog/rdb-sql) are common. In business strategy, expect questions on [SWOT, 3C, and PEST](/blog/swot-3c-pest) and [business models](/blog/business-models). For more details, check out our [Summary of Exam Trends Over the Past 5 Years](/blog/recent-trends).

## Guidelines for What to Skip

For efficient studying, you need the courage to skip certain topics. Minor laws and outdated technologies have a low appearance frequency, so feel free to exclude them from your study plan. Also, avoid spending too much time on complex calculation problems like PERT charts; it's wiser to postpone them.

## Cautions for the Two-Week Plan

To succeed with the two-week plan, managing your health is the most important factor. Avoid studying by cutting into your sleep time, as it will be counterproductive. It's also effective to schedule one rest day with no studying to give your brain a break. If you ever feel anxious, remember that the passing line is 60%.

## If You Have More Than Two Weeks

If you have a longer study period than two weeks, consider a one-month or three-month plan. For a one-month plan, see [The Complete 1-Month Plan to Pass the IT Passport](/blog/study-1month-plan); for a three-month plan, refer to [The Working Professional's Roadmap: Pass in 30 Minutes a Day](/blog/study-30min-daily).

## Summary

If you can secure 60 hours of study time and focus on frequently tested topics through past-exam-centered learning, passing in two weeks is entirely achievable. Managing your health and having the courage to skip low-yield topics are the keys to success. Use this site's [list of past exams](/exams) and [mock exams](/exam) to engage in focused practice.
