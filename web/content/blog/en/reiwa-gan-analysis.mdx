## Prerequisites for the Heisei 31 / Reiwa 1 Exams

2019 was the year of the era name change. The first half (spring) was administered as Heisei 31, and the second half (fall) as Reiwa 1. The spring exam consisted of 100 questions with the exam code 2019h31h. The fall exam also consisted of 100 questions with the exam code 2019r01a. A total of 200 questions were analyzed.

## Distribution of Questions by Field (2019)

| Field | Spring + Fall | Percentage |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Strategy | 70 questions | 35% |
| Management | 40 questions | 20% |
| Technology | 90 questions | 45% |

The 35:20:45 ratio was maintained throughout both the spring and fall 2019 exams. This same balance continues in the year-round exams from Reiwa 3 onward.

## Number of Questions by Keyword in 2019

Security had 10 questions, making it the most frequently tested field in 2019 as well. Project management had 8 questions, the highest number in the past five years. IoT recorded a high of 7 questions, on par with the high levels seen in Reiwa 3 through 7. Cryptography had 4 questions, the most in the past five years. Agile also had 4 questions, setting a five-year high. AI was relatively low at 3 questions, reflecting the period before generative AI became widespread. RPA had 2 questions, during its early stages. Authentication had 2 questions, personal information had 1, and Cloud/BCP had 0 questions.

## Characteristics of the 2019 Exams

### The "Spring/Fall" System Before Year-Round CBT

Up until the 2019 academic year, the exam was administered as a fixed twice-yearly event (spring and fall). 2020 was an unusual year, with only an October administration due to COVID-19. From 2021, it was converted to a year-round CBT format.

### High Number of Project Management Questions

Project management had 8 questions, the highest in the past five years. The questions focused on WBS, PERT, stakeholders, and risk management. For preparation, it is recommended to study [PMBOK, WBS, and Gantt Charts](/blog/pmbok-wbs) and [Agile Development and Scrum](/blog/agile-scrum).

### Cryptography and Agile at a Five-Year High

Cryptography had 4 questions, covering the basics of public keys, private keys, and hashing. Agile also had 4 questions, focusing on Scrum, XP, and pair programming. [Basics of Encryption](/blog/encryption-basics) and [Agile Development and Scrum](/blog/agile-scrum) are helpful for preparation.

### Few AI Questions

In 2019, there were only 3 AI-related questions. This was before the era of generative AI (ChatGPT was released in 2022), and the focus was on the basics of machine learning.

### IoT Frequently Tested from an Early Stage

IoT was already a major topic with 7 questions in 2019, showing that the trend was well-established.
