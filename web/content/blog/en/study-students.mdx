## The Value of Students Getting the IT Passport

### Advantage in Job Hunting

The IT Passport leads to being evaluated as "a student who understands IT fundamentals," even at companies outside the IT industry. Some major companies and civil service exams offer extra points or preferential treatment, and it serves as a strong selling point as a national certification (ITSS Level 1) you can list on your resume.

### Evaluation in Recommendation and AO Admissions

For high school students, there are many cases where the IT Passport is used in university comprehensive selection or designated school recommendation systems. It is effective in application documents and interviews as evidence of "a self-directed learning attitude."

### University Credit Recognition

Some universities have systems that grant credits to IT Passport holders. For students entering information technology departments, it is also positioned as a stepping stone to the Fundamental Information Technology Engineer Examination (FE).

### Lifelong Useful Knowledge

You acquire essential modern knowledge such as DX, security, and AI, which is practical content you can use immediately in the workplace after getting a job.

## Benefits for Students

### You Have Time

You can study intensively between classes or during long breaks (summer and spring vacations). Aiming to pass with one month of focused study is realistic.

### You Are Familiar with IT

As a native generation that uses smartphones and PCs daily, you quickly get accustomed to terminology and can naturally use AI tools.

### Learning Resources Are Cheap

There are abundant free past exam question sites and YouTube videos, keeping costs low. You can also use student discount systems to reduce the exam fee. For details, check [IT Passport Student Discount and Exam Fee Reduction](/blog/exam-discount).

## Sample Schedules for Students

### High School Student (1-Month Intensive)

By studying 1 hour on weekdays and 4 hours on weekends for 4 weeks, you can achieve a total study time of about 50 hours. The summer vacation of a third-year high school student who has finished club activities is a prime target.

### College Student (2-Week Summer Intensive)

Studying 4 hours a day for 14 days gives you about 60 hours of study time, making short-term passing possible. A detailed study plan is introduced in [2-Week Intensive Plan to Pass](/blog/study-2weeks-plan).

### Using Commute Time (3 Months)

Using a past exam question app on your smartphone for 30 minutes a day on the train, continuing for 90 days, secures about 50 hours of study time.

## Strategies for Students

### Strategy 1: Get It Before Starting Job Hunting

If you obtain the IT Passport by the spring of your third year of college, you can list it on your resume. It can also be used as material for "gakuchika" (things you worked hard on during your student days) in job hunting.

### Strategy 2: Learn Management and Marketing in the Strategy Domain

This aligns well with knowledge learned in university management or economics courses. You can practically understand frameworks like [SWOT, 3C, PEST](/blog/swot-3c-pest) and [Marketing Mix](/blog/marketing-mix) through exam preparation.

### Strategy 3: Build IT Literacy in the Technology Domain

Fields like programming, AI, and security are directly connected to practical work as knowledge of [AI and Machine Learning Basics](/blog/ai-ml-basics) and [Cyber Attacks](/blog/cyber-attacks).

## Next Steps

### Toward the Fundamental Information Technology Engineer Examination (FE)

The typical next step after the IT Passport (Level 1) is the Fundamental Information Technology Engineer Examination (Level 2). If you aspire to the IT industry, it is desirable to obtain the FE as well. The differences between the two are compared in [IT Passport vs. FE](/blog/it-vs-fe).

### Applied Information Technology Engineer Examination (AP) and Advanced Exams

If you aim for an IT specialist career, you can also challenge the higher-level Applied Information Technology Engineer Examination (AP) or advanced exams.

## Experience Patterns of Successful Student Test-Takers

### First-Year College Student (Liberal Arts)

There is a case of passing with a 2-week intensive study during spring break and receiving high praise during job hunting.

### Third-Year High School Student

There are reports of students passing one month after finishing club activities and listing the certification in their AO admission application documents.
