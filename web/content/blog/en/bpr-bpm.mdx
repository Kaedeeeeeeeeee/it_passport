## The Difference Between BPR and BPM

BPR stands for Business Process Reengineering, a method for radically redesigning business processes. BPM, on the other hand, stands for Business Process Management, which refers to the continuous improvement and management of business processes. These two approaches are used in stages: first, BPR is applied for a major overhaul, followed by BPM for ongoing daily operations and incremental improvements.

## Characteristics of BPR

BPR is a methodology pioneered by Michael Hammer and others in the United States during the 1990s. It involves re-examining existing operations from a zero base and leveraging IT to achieve dramatic efficiency gains. BPR often involves restructuring or organizational reorganization, and because there are many failure cases, careful planning is essential.

### Typical Examples of BPR

A common application of BPR is the digitization of paper-based operations. Integrating processes across departments and centralizing customer service (front-office integration) are also typical examples. These reforms fundamentally improve business processes from the ground up.

## Characteristics of BPM

BPM is a management methodology for continuously analyzing, improving, and automating business processes. It plays the role of solidifying the reforms achieved through BPR and then layering on further improvements. Tools called BPMS (BPM Suites) are used to design, execute, and monitor process flows.

### Components of BPM

A key component of BPM is process modeling. This involves using BPMN notation to diagram flows, followed by execution and automation. Furthermore, a cycle of monitoring, analysis, and linking results back to improvement is crucial.

## Related Methodologies

### RPA (Robotic Process Automation)

RPA is a technology that automates routine tasks using software robots. It is often used in the automation phase of BPM and contributes to operational efficiency.

### Workflow Systems

Workflow systems are mechanisms that digitize application and approval processes. They are commonly introduced as part of a BPM initiative.

## Key Points for the IT Passport Exam

The difference between BPR and BPM is a frequent topic on the exam. Remember the contrast: BPR is for radical reform, while BPM is for continuous improvement. Questions also often cover the relationship with RPA, so be sure to understand that as well.

### Typical Past Exam Question Patterns

- "Which method involves radically redesigning business processes?" → BPR
- "Which method involves continuously managing and improving business processes?" → BPM

## Study Tips

It's easier to distinguish BPR and BPM by remembering their translations: BPR is "reengineering" (redesign), while BPM is "management" (control). Contrast them with the verbs "dramatic/radical" for BPR and "continuous" for BPM. Also, keeping the sequence of BPR → BPM in mind will help with exam questions.
